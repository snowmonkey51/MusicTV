How It Works

Everything you need to get MusicTV running — from first launch to streaming on your TV.

Quick Start

Up and running in minutes.

MusicTV is designed to stay out of the way. Point it at your videos and it does the rest.

1

Choose your folder

Open MusicTV on your Mac and select the folder where your music videos live. That's all the setup required.

2

Share your library

Flip on "Share Library" and your Mac becomes a local streaming server. Discoverable instantly on your network.

3

Connect on Apple TV

Open MusicTV on your Apple TV. Your Mac appears in the list. Tap to connect and your library loads in seconds.

4

Sit back and watch

MusicTV shuffles and plays your collection in a continuous loop. Your own music video channel, on your TV.

Organizing Your Library

Folders are your genres

MusicTV reads your folder structure and uses it as your genre system. Every folder you create inside your library becomes a genre you can browse and filter by — no tagging or metadata required.

Nesting works too. A subfolder inside a genre folder creates a sub-genre, and MusicTV tracks both levels so you can filter broadly or get specific.

📁 Music Videos/
  📁 Rock/
    📁 Classic Rock/
      🎬 Led Zeppelin - Kashmir.mp4
    📁 Alternative/
      🎬 Radiohead - Karma Police.mp4
  📁 Hip-Hop/
    🎬 Kendrick Lamar - HUMBLE.mp4
  📁 Pop/
    🎬 Dua Lipa - Levitating.mp4

In the sidebar on your Mac, each top-level folder appears as a genre. Tap a genre on Apple TV to filter playback to just those videos. Sub-genre folders narrow it down further.

Files sitting directly in the root of your library — not inside any folder — are treated as uncategorized and always included in the full shuffle.

Video Filters

MusicTV applies real-time GPU-accelerated filters to every frame as your videos play. Switch filters any time from the sidebar on Mac or the settings panel on Apple TV — no restart needed.

What it does

Adds animated scanlines, phosphor glow, and subtle screen curvature to every frame — like watching on a vintage cathode-ray TV. The grain and scanline pattern shifts in real time so it never looks static.

Best for

80s and 90s music videos, retro aesthetics, or any time you want your channel to feel like late-night MTV. The most visually distinctive filter in MusicTV.

What it does

Converts every frame to true monochrome using Apple's Photo Effect Mono pipeline. Colors are stripped and luminance is preserved — the result is clean black and white, not just desaturated grey.

Best for

Classic or art-house videos, late-night ambient viewing, or whenever you want a timeless, cinematic look that doesn't compete with the music.

What it does

Fades the colors, adds a warm tint, and darkens the edges with a soft vignette. It's a three-layer effect — fade, color grade, and vignette — all applied per frame in real time.

Best for

Giving modern videos an analog warmth, or leaning into the look of videos that were already shot on film. A subtler effect than CRT — great as an always-on choice.

Network Streaming Setup

Stream your Mac library to any Apple TV on your network — no cables, no subscriptions, no internet required. Everything travels over your local Wi-Fi.

1

Choose your library folder

Open MusicTV on your Mac and click Choose Folder in the sidebar to select the folder where your music videos live. This only needs to be done once.

2

Turn on Share Library

Click the gear icon in the Mac sidebar to open Settings. Toggle on Share Library. MusicTV immediately begins broadcasting your library over your local network.

3

Open MusicTV on your Apple TV

Launch MusicTV on your Apple TV. The landing screen automatically scans your network for shared libraries — no setup or IP addresses required.

4

Tap your Mac in the list

Your Mac appears by name. Tap it to connect. MusicTV downloads your library index — including all videos, favorites, bumpers, and settings — directly from your Mac.

5

Playback begins

Your playlist builds and videos start playing. All video files stream on demand from your Mac over Wi-Fi — nothing is copied to your Apple TV.

Requirement: Your Mac and Apple TV must be on the same Wi-Fi network. No internet connection is used — everything stays local and private.

Voice Search on Apple TV

The Siri Remote's microphone button doubles as a fast-search shortcut inside MusicTV. Jump to any artist or song without typing a single character.

1

Swipe down during playback

While a video is playing, swipe down on the Siri Remote trackpad to open the player info panel.

2

Go to the Search tab

Navigate to the Search tab at the top of the panel using the Siri Remote trackpad.

3

Focus the search field

Move focus to the "Artist or song…" text field and press the trackpad center to select it.

4

Press the mic button and speak

Press the microphone button on your Siri Remote. tvOS handles speech recognition — your spoken words appear directly in the search field. Say an artist name, song title, or genre.

5

Select a result to play

MusicTV filters your library as your words appear. Navigate to the video you want and press the trackpad to jump straight to it.

Tip: Partial names work — saying "Led" will surface all Led Zeppelin videos. Search covers artist names, song titles, and genre folder names.

Favorites & Filtering

Star the videos you love and filter your playlist down to just those. Combine favorites with genre filtering to build exactly the channel you want.

Marking Favorites

Star videos on your Mac

Click the star button while a video is playing to mark it as a favorite. You can also open the Favorites panel in the Mac sidebar to view, play, and remove starred videos — organized by artist.

Filtering by Favorites

Play only your favorites

In the Mac sidebar playlist picker, select Favorites to rebuild the playlist using only your starred videos. The filtered playlist streams to any connected Apple TV automatically.

Genre Filtering

Focus on a genre

Click any folder name in the playlist picker to limit playback to videos in that genre. Your folder structure is your filter system — no file tagging or metadata required.

Network Sync

Stays in sync everywhere

Favorites sync bidirectionally over your local network. Star a video on your Mac while Apple TV is connected and it updates instantly — and any changes on Apple TV push back to your Mac in real time.

Bumper Videos

Bumpers are short clips — logo animations, channel IDs, interstitials — that play between music videos. They make long shuffle sessions feel like a real broadcast channel.

Setup

Choose a bumper folder

In the Mac sidebar, click Choose Bumper Folder and select a folder of short video clips. MusicTV treats every video in that folder as a bumper.

Settings → Playback

Bumper every N videos

Use the Bumper Interval stepper to control how often bumpers appear — from every video (1) to every 50. The default is every 5 music videos. Bumpers cycle through the folder and won't repeat back-to-back.

Settings → Playback

Show & Shuffle Bumpers

Toggle Show Bumpers off to temporarily suppress all interstitials without losing your folder selection. Toggle Shuffle Bumpers on to randomize the order instead of playing them alphabetically.

Settings → Appearance

Opening bumper

Pick a single clip under Intro to play once at the very start of each session, before the first music video. Great for a channel logo or title card. Mac only — Apple TV receives bumpers streamed over the network.
